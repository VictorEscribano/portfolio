Computer Vision · One-day build

Tennis Movement Analysis

Turning a broadcast tennis feed into a top-view movement and heatmap analysis of both players.

YOLOOpenCVHomographyTracking

Overview

A one-day computer-vision project inspired by the US Open final between Carlos Alcaraz and Casper Ruud. A YOLO network detects both players in the broadcast feed and classifies them into their respective regions of the court.

Using planar homographies, the broadcast perspective is warped into a clean top-view of the court, where each player's position is plotted over time — turning a TV feed into a movement and density analysis of how the two players covered the court.

Demo

Player tracking and top-view court projection in motion

Highlights

  • YOLO player detection with region-of-interest classification
  • Planar homography projecting broadcast view to a top-view court
  • Per-player movement trails and density heatmaps
  • Built end-to-end in a single day
